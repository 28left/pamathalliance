About

The Pennsylvania Math Alliance (PAMath) is a regional alliance in the mathematical sciences covering the all of Pennsylvania. Such a regional alliance concept has been built upon the successful National Math Alliance (National Alliance for Doctoral Studies in the Mathematical Sciences). The mentors in the alliance help to establish connections with other faculty across Pennsylvania who will work closely with PAMath toward the goals of the National Math Alliance:

1.

Work closely with the National Math Alliance with the ultimate goal of substantially increasing annual PhD production in the mathematical sciences for URM (under-represented minorities).

2.

To build a regional community that will help students in the mathematical sciences negotiate critical transitions during college. This will be done by establishing a strong network of faculty in key institutions and gradually spreading the network to more institutions. These faculty members will work together to recruit new students each year from Pennsylvania institutions of higher learning include HBCUs, community colleges, primarily undergraduate institutions and research universities. The PAMath will also work closely with the National Math Alliance to help later to transition those recruited students who are interested in graduate studies to graduate programs in the mathematical sciences.

3.

To facilitate the efforts so that the students at Pennsylvania institutions can be involved in undergraduate research experiences. The PAMath will help to provide infrastructure and ideas and to bring the working parties together.

4.

To build a community across Pennsylvania. The targeted student population will be students who have little contact with institutions offering graduate studies in the mathematical sciences. We expect that about 80% of such students will be URMs.

5.

To provide support and resources for alliance members. For example, to recruit new mentors and strengthen current mentors, to strengthen collaboration among alliance members, and to expand opportunities for alliance faculty and students.

We are motivated by and work in the spirit of Professor Federico Ardila's four axioms:

  1. Axiom 1. Mathematical potential is equally present in different groups, irrespective of geographic, demographic, and economic boundaries.
  2. Axiom 2. Everyone can have joyful, meaningful, and empowering mathematical experiences.
  3. Axiom 3. Mathematics is a powerful, malleable tool that can be shaped and used differently by various communities to serve their needs.
  4. Axiom 4. Every student deserves to be treated with dignity and respect.

The PA Math Alliance's goals are to make mathematics more accessible for students. Here we give some detail as to how we do that.

Students in their first and second year

  • What does the PA Math Alliance offer me? Our annual conference will give you the chance to meet math faculty and students from all over PA. You'll be able to learn about research and internship opportunities, including some that are appropriate for students at the start of the mathematical journeys.
  • How do I get involved? Attend the conference (more information here) and email us at info@pamathalliance.org.

Students in their third and fourth year

  • What does the PA Math Alliance offer me? At our annual conference you can meet representatives from REUs, industry and graduate programs. Also, PAMath can help connect you to the National Math Alliance who are very experienced with helping people interested in applying to graduate school.
  • How do I get involved? Attend the conference (more information here) and email us at info@pamathalliance.org. Also, consider signing up to be a Alliance Scholar at the National Math Alliance's webpage here.

If you're interested in helping undergraduate students have a more enriching mathematical experience and if you'd like to help them connect to resources such as research opportunities, interships and graudate programs, you might consider becoming a mentor for the Pennsylvania Math Alliance. Our mentors come from a variety of institutions (community colleges, PhD granting institutions, primarily undergraduate institutions, etc) but what they have in common is a commitment in bringing together students, faculty and STEM leaders from across Pennsylvania.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is the Pennsylvania Math Alliance?

    A network of faculty, students and STEM leaders from across Pennsylvania committed to broadening and strengthening that network.

  • What is the Pennsylvania Math Alliance's relationship to the National Math Alliance?

    Our goals overlap but while the National Math Alliance is focused on students who are about to graduate or are in graduate school, the PA Math Alliance is focused on students who are at an earlier stage of their mathematical journey.

  • How established is the National Math Alliance?

    Go here to see some data and charts that show how the National Alliance has been growing and the impact it has had on its scholars.

  • What are examples of specific resources you can share with students?

    While the representatives at the conference change each year, there are other resources that we can point students towards outside the conference:

    • Internship opportunities for students
    • Research experiences for undergraduates
    • Information about careers that use the mathematical sciences
